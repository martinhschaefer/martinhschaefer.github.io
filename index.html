computational cancer biology

We are interested how mutations or modifications of the DNA transform a healthy into a cancer cell. This transformation is an evolutionary process in which cells vary, compete and the fittest survive. This happens in an environment, which modulates mutational processes and the fittness effect of each DNA alteration. Conversely, also the tumor affects its environment: it needs to create a microenvironment that is favourable for tumor progression. We study those tumor-microenvironment interactions in both directions with the goal to create a systems understanding of tumor evolution in a complex ecosystem of cells.

The tools we are using to achieve this are from the fields of population genetics, systems biology, machine learning and network science. Our computational cancer biology lab is located at the IEO in Milan.
